Coin Sniper

  • Originality: Unlike some "click the objects that appear" games, this one continually adds "hazard" circles that end the game once clicked, creating a bullet hell feel.
  • Technical Merit: True to the challenge I chose to use, no objects are ever destroyed over the course of play. Coins move after being clicked, and every coin click spawns a new hazard.
  • Prototype Postmortem: I learned that click-and-drag support in Phaser is sketchy at best, forcing me to abandon the old idea I had in favor of something simpler and easier to implement. Setting up randomized position and direction was easy, but getting screen wrap was a workaround for bouncing off the screen bounds not working as I wanted.
  • Prototype Assets:
    • Red and yellow circles "drawn" by me
    • velocityFromAngle for getting angle vectors idea from here
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: The game is nearest to "clicker"-type idle games early on, but as the game progresses it starts to resemble bullet hell games like Touhou.
  • High Concept: Click coins and avoid red dots.
  • Theme: "Things must only be added as the game progresses, not removed."
  • Mandated Variety: Mouse, a lot of randomness, Collector, puzzle
  • Prototype Goal: Test precision clicking as more and more of the game screen becomes dangerous to click on.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want them to have a level of confidence when few coins are in play, quickly degrading into panic as the hazards continue multiplying and their score grows ever higher.
  • Gameplay: The controls are extremely simple: use the mouse to click on coins as they fly across the playfield. Every time a coin is clicked on, it moves elsewhere and a new hazard appears. Clicking a hazard instantly ends the game and displays your score. Refresh the page to play again.
  • Strategies: Patience and precision are the key strategies for playing this game effectively.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: This is an abstract game, relying on easily recognizable shapes (coins = good) and colors (red = danger). There's no particular narrative to this game, but I made sure to make the gameplay fun and potentially addictive to make up for it.
  • Target Audience: Players who want something simple, yet challenging should enjoy this game.
  • Play Time: Sessions could theoretically go on forever, but at most a session should take 3-5 minutes.
