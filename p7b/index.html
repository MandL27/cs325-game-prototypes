Power Maze

  • Originality: While many bridge puzzle-type games exist, Power Maze adds a greater element of planning ahead by only allowing certain paths to be unlocked (and re-locked) from one side. Players are thus unable to move theoretically infinitely with a single bridge.
  • Technical Merit: I implemented a semi-formal object definition to track room data, like which sides have doors and whether locks are accessible from that side.
  • Prototype Postmortem: My biggest lesson from this prototype is that JS is an iffy-at-best language for OOP. In other languages (like C#), I could easily whip up a class for room data; however, here I used a more struct-like type that felt hacky at best. This may just be me being unfamiliar with JS, though.

  • Prototype Closest Other Game: While I was unable to add multiple levels, this iteration of Power Maze features a much larger (and harder!) maze introducing more advanced techniques.
  • High Concept: Use keys and locks (some one-sided, some dual-sided) to navigate through a small maze.
  • Prototype Goal: As this prototype stands now, it represents a single level in the hypothetical finished game. It also covers many of the techniques I would want to introduce to players.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want this game to force players to think through the scenario before them. Like many puzzle games, Power Maze caters to mind-oriented gamers.
  • Gameplay: The gameplay is simple: use the arrow keys to move between rooms and WASD to insert or remove keys. The objective is to reach the checkered room in the corner opposite where you start.
  • Strategies: Long-term planning and recognizing necessary conditions are the order of the day in Power Maze. Being able to think through a plan is the key to success.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: This whole game is abstract with no real narrative component, though players are free to interpret it as a prison break or other type of escape scenario.
  • Target Audience: Puzzle fans who want a new take on bridge puzzle games.
  • Play Time: The game should take no longer than five minutes to play.
